About us

By Adrien and Melanie September 23, 2020

(by Adrien)

Mel discovered the joy of cycling much later than I did, but caught the travel bug long before me üêû. We met in secondary school at the bottom of a climbing wall and quickly became very good friends. Then, each of us went a different way in University and we lost contact for a while until we met again 5 years later, and stuck together for good that time. By that time she'd travelled to 3 different continents and had a passport full of fancy stamps, while I barely left France. After she finished medical school and graduated as a psychiatrist, we convinced each other to travel around Asia for 6 months. She pushed for us to spend 2 months volunteering in a Nepali school, which turned out to be the highlight of the trip. Moving to England, was a much more complicated task for her than it was for me. Doctors in France don't learn a lot of English and her speciality implies that you talk to patients about very complex issues. Pretty tough when it is not your native language. But she bravely took on the challenge, and before we knew, she was a consultant at the NHS. In 2018, she gave me a beautiful little boy, our little star.¬†

‚óã Adrien

(by Melanie)

I would never thank enough Adrien for pushing me to embed cycling in my daily life. It is such a great way of moving and also travelling. It was probably hard work but Adrien is a very convincing and tenacious person. There are many things I love in Adrien. He is one of the rare persons I know with great values such as honesty, curiosity, openness to new ideas (even though you need to have often strong arguments to convince him when he has already done his own extensive research), being ready to stand up and act for important matters (particularly climate change, girls education, inequalities). He is the practical and realistic side of the couple, which sometimes I fight against, but I must admit it is very reassuring and comforting. Always optimistic, with plan B (and C, and D, ..), which is very useful while on the road when you have a mishaps. His amazing (almost perfect) sense of direction is also very helpful, so are his shortcuts (although I won't tell about a couple of stories such as lost in a river at dusk without light ...). He is obviously an amazing daddy to our little one and Oscar is very lucky to have a dad that teaches him so many great things such as riding a bike when he was not even 3 yo.¬†

‚òÜ Oscar

(by Mummy and Daddy)

Our little boy has been on the wheels pretty much since he was born. He is super enthusiastic and easy going during our trips. It is such a fantastic journey to follow him as he grows and is becoming confident, funny, curious and clever.¬† We are dreaming to live a big cycle adventure as a family and Oscar is one of our main reasons for this Journey. We can buy lots of things in life, but we cannot buy time (as far as we know), and the time with Oscar is gold. While we are on the road, for short or longer trips, we want to teach him important values such as open-mindedness, confidence, curiosity, patience... Obviously, sometimes mishaps happens when you are on the road with a a young kid, however it is clearly balanced with numerous enjoyable moments and positive impacts.
