Getting ready for the Highlands and the Outer Hebrides

By Adrien and Melanie October 1, 2020 Scotland 2020

Planned itinerary overview

The Hebridean way was on our list of near mythical route to ride for quite some time. But, the north coast of Spain was also quite high on that list... Well, at least COVID19 helped us to decide where to go for our first big cycle touring adventure.

Last year we cycled for 10 days in Cornwall, but at a relatively relaxed pace (although some sections were steep as hell), on tarmac and with a glorious weather. It was a fantastic holiday, but we took it easy because Oscar was only 1 year old. 

This year we have brand new custom Niner RLT9 gravel bikes assembled by Mike from Stage1Cycle. Oscar is two and a half and very much used to spend hours in his tout-terrain trailer without complaining too much. We planned 600 km with a fair amount of bumpy trails and a few remote mountainous passes. Our planned itinerary starts from the city of Stirling, easily accessible by train and will get us through the magical lochs of the Trossachs before reaching Oban. From there we will board a ferry to little island of Barra. During nearly a week, we will cycle North through Eriksay, South and North Uist, Benbecula, Grimsay, Berneray and finally Lewis and Harris. Then we will cross the sea back to Ullapool on the mainland and will slowly join Inverness, where our tour will end. Well at least that's our plan...

COVID19 means planning everything 

Because of COVID, we decided to plan most of the nights, in particular on the Hebrides where most of the BnBs and campsites are still closed for business until 2021. We just kept a few nights for wild camping or last minute B&B. 

We tried to stay on a low  budget
